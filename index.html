Ender's Game

Soham Shah

A quick background on the story

Humanity has been attacked by an alien race which we refer to as the buggers or the Formics

The losses were devestating

However we're able to fight off the first wave of the aliens

The world is now run by one central hegemony. In order to prepare, the government takes the best and brightest students into space at a place which they call the battle school to train the commanders of the next fleet.

The battle school is where children are taught through a series of war games how to strategize and the best and brightest are promoted.

The Central Conflict

The Battle School chldren are manipulated, into commanding the humans fleet to fight the buggers and kill them off

There were no games, the battles were real, and the only enemy you fought was the buggers. You won every battle, and today you finally fought them at their home world, where the queen was, all the queens from all their colonies, they all were there and you destroyed them completely. Questions this raises

Is it morally justifiable for humanity to kill off another species because we don't understand them or can't communicate with them?

What kinds of buggers do we kill off today? Either through willful ignorance or purposeful destruction.

  • Race
  • Religion
  • Ethnicity
  • Gender

Do the ends justify the means? Is it okay to use child soldiers if we win the battle?
